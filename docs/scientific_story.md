# Scientific Story: CsPbBr3 ARPES Mass And The Polaron Question

> Does the ARPES-observed hole effective mass in CsPbBr3 constitute evidence for large-polaron mass dressing, or can the same mass scale be explained by finite-temperature disorder, quasiparticle baselines, or experimental/modeling protocol differences?

## Experimental Anchor

The graph is anchored by a room-temperature ARPES measurement on orthorhombic CsPbBr3. The valence-band dispersion is fitted near the valence-band maximum, giving:

> m_exp = 0.26 +/- 0.02 m_e

This is treated as a measurement fact, not yet as a mechanism claim. The story begins when different theories explain the same mass in different ways.

## Competing Explanations

The polaron branch says the measured mass is evidence for electron-phonon dressing:

- A Fröhlich electron-phonon coupling model supplies a coupling parameter and bare mass.
- A Feynman variational model renormalizes the bare hole mass to about 0.24 m_e.
- This falls within the ARPES uncertainty interval, so the mass enhancement is interpreted as a large-polaron signature.

The finite-temperature branch says the same mass can arise without carrier self-localization:

- AIMD samples CsPbBr3 at 300 K.
- HSE-SOC calculations on a representative thermal structure give a hole mass near 0.265 m0.
- This matches the ARPES-scale mass without adding an extra hole.
- Explicit extra-hole calculations do not produce localized in-gap states or a strong valence-band perturbation under the simulated bulk-like conditions.

The G0W0/re-measurement branch challenges the mass-as-polaron-signature inference from another direction:

- A separate ARPES analysis reports a lower hole mass, about 0.203 +/- 0.016 m0.
- Orthorhombic G0W0 with spin-orbit coupling gives a hole mass near 0.226 m0.
- This suggests that a quasiparticle baseline can account for the measured mass without extra Fröhlich-polaron renormalization.

None of these branches simply rejects ARPES. They disagree about what ARPES proves.

## Scientific Tension

The current graph contains one accepted contradiction:

> ARPES effective-mass enhancement is a valid signature of polaronic mass dressing, even without resolved phonon replicas.
>
> vs.
>
> The ARPES-observed effective mass can be explained by a G0W0 quasiparticle baseline and does not require additional Fröhlich-polaron renormalization.

This is not just a numerical disagreement. It is a disagreement over the evidential status of an observable: whether fitted ARPES effective mass is itself a polaron signature, or whether it only becomes meaningful after comparison with matched finite-temperature and quasiparticle baselines.

## Takeaway

The current graph says:

> The CsPbBr3 ARPES mass is a useful benchmark, but effective mass alone underdetermines the microscopic mechanism.

The unresolved discriminator is what should count as decisive evidence: phonon replicas, carrier-induced localization, temperature dependence, surface/bulk consistency, or a theory baseline that includes the right finite-temperature structure and quasiparticle corrections.
